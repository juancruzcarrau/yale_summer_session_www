Cultural shock in Yale Summer Session

Being an international student in the Yale Summer Session has had many surprises. The interaction with people from other cultures as similar or radically different of my own has been quite shocking but no less funny. Many are the topics that I could work on but I will focus on the main ones I have experienced.

Greetings

While it is accustomed in the USA and asian countries to greet with a handshake to someone who is not that close to you, in Uruguay (and most of Latin America) it is customary to greet women with a kiss in the cheeks if you have met them once or twice and you have an informal relationtip.

Food

Uruguay, being one of the biggest meat exporters and eaters in the whole world, we are quite used to eat plain meat, more specifically cooked in a grill. Also, the less fat the meat has, the better we consider it.Furthermore, food here has much more butter and grease than at home so it tends to be harder on the stomach. Moreover, one thing we Latin people have had to get accustomed to is eating dinner so early as we tend to have it around 21:00!

Accents

As Spanish is one of the most spoken languages, it was a fact that I would run into other Spanish speakers. So far I've met Mexicans, Spaniards, Argentinians and Puerto Ricans, each one of them with a different accent! Although the argentinian accent is quite similar to my own, there are some subtle differences. However, with all of the other ones I never thought I would see such contrasting pronounciations, distinctive vocabulary, and general diversity all in all.

Communication

This is a bit of a weird thing for everyone in Latin America as well as Europeans which is the use of Whatsapp. We've come to the realization that most Americans just use iMessagge (given that Apple has taken over the world in here...) and the Chinese use WeChat.

College Lifestyle

Living in a College has been one of the best experiences for me as I live in my house in my home country. Seeing my classmates and friends all day long is so different to what I am used to but is find it so fun. In addition to this, we all find it so incredible how many activities are organized by the college offices for the studens.

Sale Tax

It might be a very minor detail for some people, but we foreigners find it so annoying. We like knowing what we are going to pay for the products we are about to buy, and we also feel kind of decieved by the lower price shown on the tag.
